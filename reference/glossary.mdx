This glossary defines the terms and shorthand used throughout Project Notes. Keeping language consistent across all your notes makes them easier to scan and search — especially when you return to a project after weeks away.

## A

**Action Item** — A specific task assigned to a person with a due date, typically captured during a meeting.

**Active** — A project currently in progress with regular updates.

## B

**Blocker** — An issue preventing progress on a task or project until resolved.

## D

**Decision Log** — A running record of significant choices made during a project, including the rationale.

**Discovery** — An early project phase focused on research and requirements gathering before execution begins.

## L

**LGTM** — "Looks good to me." A shorthand approval, often used in code review or async sign-off.

## M

**MVP** — Minimum Viable Product. The smallest version of something that delivers value.

## O

**On Hold** — A project paused due to resource constraints, priority shifts, or external blockers.

## P

**POC** — Proof of Concept. A small test to validate an idea before committing to full development.

## R

**Retrospective (Retro)** — A meeting held after a project or sprint to reflect on what went well and what to improve.

## S

**Spike** — A short, time-boxed research task to reduce uncertainty before starting main work.

**Status** — The current state of a project: Active, On Hold, or Complete.

## T

**TBD** — To Be Determined. Marks decisions or dates not yet finalized.

## W

**WIP** — Work In Progress. Indicates something is actively being worked on but not yet complete.

<Note>
  This glossary reflects personal conventions, not industry standards. Adjust terms to match your team's or your own preferred vocabulary.
</Note>
