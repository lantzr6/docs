Use this template for any meeting where you want a record of what was discussed and what happens next. Fill it in during the call — capturing action items in real time prevents things from falling through the cracks.

```markdown meeting-notes-template.md
# Meeting: [Topic]

**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD  
**Time:** HH:MM – HH:MM  
**Attendees:** Name 1, Name 2, Name 3  
**Facilitator:** Your Name  

## Agenda

1. Item 1
2. Item 2
3. Item 3

## Notes

### Item 1

- Key point
- Key point

### Item 2

- Key point

## Decisions Made

- Decision 1
- Decision 2

## Action Items

| Action | Owner | Due Date |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Task description | Name | YYYY-MM-DD |

## Next Meeting

- **Date:** TBD
- **Goal:** 
```

## Tips for Better Meeting Notes

- Send the agenda at least 24 hours before so attendees can come prepared.
- Write decisions in plain language, not shorthand — notes need to make sense weeks later.
- Assign every action item to a specific person with a due date so nothing falls through the cracks.
- Share the notes within 24 hours while context is fresh and corrections are easy to make.
- Review open action items at the start of the next meeting to keep momentum and accountability.
