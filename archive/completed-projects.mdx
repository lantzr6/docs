This page archives finished projects. Move a project here once it's complete — it keeps your active Projects section clean while preserving the history and lessons from past work.

## How to Archive a Project

<Steps>
  <Step title="Mark it complete">
    Change the project status to ✅ Complete
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write an Outcome section">
    Add a final **Outcome** section to the project page with 2–3 sentences on what was delivered.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Capture lessons learned">
    Add a **Lessons Learned** section with bullet points reflecting on what went well and what you'd do differently.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a table entry">
    Add an entry to the table below with a link to the project page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove from active navigation">
    Remove it from the active Projects navigation group in your sidebar config.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Lessons Learned Bank

A distilled collection of recurring lessons from past projects — review these before kicking off something new.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Start with a user research phase" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Projects that skip discovery consistently require costly rework later. Even a week of upfront research saves weeks of revision.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Freeze scope 2 weeks before launch" icon="lock">
    Late-stage additions introduce risk without enough time to test. Enforce a scope freeze and push new requests to a follow-up milestone.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Document decisions as they happen" icon="pen-to-square">
    Context is easy to lose. Log the reasoning behind key choices in a Decision Log at the time they're made — not during the retrospective.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Set up a staging environment early" icon="server">
    Waiting until the final stretch to set up a staging environment creates bottlenecks. Provision it at project kickoff so testing is never blocked.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
